Definition: Let $X$ be a topological space. If $\mathbb{N}$ be a sequence of points in $X$ and $n_{1} < n_{2} < \dots < \cdots$ be an increasing sequence of positive integers, then the sequence $\lbrace y_{i} \rbrace$ defined as $y_{i} = x_{n_{i}}$ is called a subsequence of the sequence $\lbrace x_{n} \rbrace$. The space $X$ is said to be sequentially compact if every sequence of points of $X$ has a convergent subsequence.
